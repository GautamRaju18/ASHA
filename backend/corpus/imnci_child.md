# IMNCI — Sick Child Age 2 Months up to 5 Years

### CHUNK
source: IMNCI Chart Booklet (Sick Child 2 months–5 years)
section: General danger signs
condition: general
age_group: child
urgency_tag: emergency
is_danger_sign: true
---
Check every sick child aged 2 months up to 5 years for general danger signs. A
child has a GENERAL DANGER SIGN if any of the following are present: the child is
not able to drink or breastfeed; the child vomits everything; the child has had
convulsions during this illness; or the child is lethargic or unconscious. A
child with any general danger sign is seriously ill and must be referred
URGENTLY to a hospital after giving pre-referral treatment. Any general danger
sign means EMERGENCY referral.

### CHUNK
source: IMNCI Chart Booklet (Sick Child 2 months–5 years)
section: Cough or difficult breathing — fast breathing thresholds
condition: pneumonia
age_group: child
urgency_tag: urgent
is_danger_sign: true
---
For a child with cough or difficult breathing, count the breaths in one minute to
check for FAST BREATHING. The cut-off depends on age: a child aged 2 months up to
12 months has fast breathing if the breathing rate is 50 breaths per minute or
more; a child aged 12 months up to 5 years has fast breathing if the rate is 40
breaths per minute or more. Fast breathing is a sign consistent with PNEUMONIA.
A child with pneumonia (fast breathing but no danger sign and no chest indrawing)
needs prompt assessment and antibiotic treatment at a health facility; refer or
treat per protocol the same day.

section: Cough or difficult breathing — severe pneumonia
condition: severe pneumonia
age_group: child
urgency_tag: emergency
is_danger_sign: true
---
A child with cough or difficult breathing who ALSO has any general danger sign,
chest indrawing (lower chest wall draws in when the child breathes in), or
stridor in a calm child is classified as SEVERE PNEUMONIA OR VERY SEVERE DISEASE.
This is an emergency. Give the first dose of an appropriate antibiotic if
available within scope and refer the child URGENTLY to hospital.

### CHUNK
source: IMNCI Chart Booklet (Sick Child 2 months–5 years)
section: Fever — when fever is dangerous
condition: fever
age_group: child
urgency_tag: urgent
is_danger_sign: true
---
A child with fever (by history, feels hot, or temperature 37.5°C or above) must
be assessed for danger signs and for stiff neck. Fever with any general danger
sign or a stiff neck is consistent with VERY SEVERE FEBRILE DISEASE (for example
meningitis or severe malaria) and requires URGENT referral. In a malaria area,
fever may be consistent with malaria; test if a rapid diagnostic test is
available and refer if the child is seriously ill. Measles with mouth ulcers,
pus from the eyes, or clouding of the cornea also needs referral.

### CHUNK
source: IMNCI Chart Booklet (Sick Child 2 months–5 years)
section: Fever — uncomplicated
condition: fever
age_group: child
urgency_tag: home
is_danger_sign: false
---
A child with fever for under 7 days who is alert, drinking well, feeding well,
and has NO general danger sign, NO stiff neck, and no other danger sign can often
be managed at home with advice: give plenty of fluids, continue feeding,
keep the child cool, and give paracetamol for high fever per weight-appropriate
dosing if within scope. Advise the caregiver on danger signs and to return
immediately if the child cannot drink, becomes more unwell, or fever lasts more
than 7 days, or to return for follow-up in 2 days if the fever persists.
