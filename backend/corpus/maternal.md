# Maternal Health — Pregnancy and Postnatal Danger Signs (ASHA / NHM)

> Starter corpus, paraphrased from public NHM/ASHA maternal danger-sign content.

### CHUNK
source: ASHA Module / NHM Maternal Health (Pregnancy danger signs)
section: Pregnancy danger signs requiring immediate referral
condition: pregnancy complication
age_group: maternal
urgency_tag: emergency
is_danger_sign: true
---
A pregnant woman with any of these danger signs must be referred IMMEDIATELY to a
PHC/CHC or hospital: vaginal bleeding; severe headache with blurred vision;
convulsions or fits; high fever; reduced or absent fetal movements; severe
abdominal pain; swelling of the face and hands; or breathlessness/severe
weakness. These are obstetric emergencies. Arrange referral transport (108) and
accompany the woman if possible.

### CHUNK
source: ASHA Module / NHM Maternal Health (Pre-eclampsia)
section: Pre-eclampsia / eclampsia warning signs
condition: pre-eclampsia
age_group: maternal
urgency_tag: emergency
is_danger_sign: true
---
Severe headache, blurred vision, swelling of the face and hands, and upper
abdominal pain in a pregnant woman are warning signs consistent with
PRE-ECLAMPSIA. If a measured blood pressure is high (for example 140/90 mmHg or
above) these signs are more concerning. A pregnant woman with convulsions has
ECLAMPSIA, which is a life-threatening emergency requiring immediate referral.

### CHUNK
source: ASHA Module / NHM Maternal Health (Anaemia)
section: Severe anaemia in pregnancy
condition: anaemia
age_group: maternal
urgency_tag: urgent
is_danger_sign: true
---
A pregnant woman who is very pale (palms, conjunctiva, tongue), breathless on
mild exertion, or excessively tired may have SEVERE ANAEMIA. Severe anaemia
(haemoglobin below 7 g/dL where measurable) needs same-day medical assessment.
Refer for evaluation; advise iron-folic acid only as part of routine programme
guidance and within scope.

### CHUNK
source: ASHA Module / NHM Maternal Health (Routine ANC)
section: Routine antenatal care guidance
condition: routine ANC
age_group: maternal
urgency_tag: routine
is_danger_sign: false
---
A pregnant woman with no danger signs should receive routine antenatal care:
ensure at least the recommended ANC check-ups, iron-folic acid and calcium
supplementation per programme, tetanus toxoid immunization, counselling on diet
and rest, birth preparedness, and institutional delivery planning. Educate her on
the danger signs that require immediate return.

### CHUNK
source: ASHA Module / NHM Maternal Health (Postnatal)
section: Postnatal danger signs (mother)
condition: postnatal complication
age_group: maternal
urgency_tag: emergency
is_danger_sign: true
---
After delivery, refer the mother immediately if she has: excessive vaginal
bleeding (soaking more than one pad in under an hour or passing large clots);
high fever with or without foul-smelling discharge; convulsions; severe headache
with blurred vision; or difficulty breathing. These postnatal danger signs are
emergencies.
