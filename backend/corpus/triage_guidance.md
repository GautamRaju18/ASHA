# Triage Category Guidance (how to map a case to the 4 levels)

> Starter corpus describing how the fixed 4-level triage labels are used. The
> assignment of a specific case is REASONED by the agent against retrieved
> protocol criteria; this chunk only explains what each label means.

### CHUNK
source: ASHA Sahayak Triage Guidance
section: Category — EMERGENCY_REFER_NOW
condition: triage policy
age_group: general
urgency_tag: emergency
is_danger_sign: false
---
Use EMERGENCY_REFER_NOW when the case shows an emergency danger sign that
threatens life within hours: a general danger sign (not able to drink/feed, vomits
everything, convulsions, lethargic/unconscious), severe pneumonia/very severe
disease, severe dehydration, a young-infant danger sign, or an obstetric
emergency. Action: refer immediately to the nearest CHC or district hospital,
arrange 108 transport, and give pre-referral first response within scope.

### CHUNK
source: ASHA Sahayak Triage Guidance
section: Category — URGENT_REFER_TODAY
condition: triage policy
age_group: general
urgency_tag: urgent
is_danger_sign: false
---
Use URGENT_REFER_TODAY when the case needs medical assessment the same day but is
not an immediate life threat: pneumonia (fast breathing without danger signs),
some dehydration not improving, persistent fever, suspected severe anaemia, or a
notifiable-disease presentation. Action: refer today to the nearest PHC/CHC and
counsel the family to go without delay.

### CHUNK
source: ASHA Sahayak Triage Guidance
section: Category — HOME_CARE_WITH_FOLLOWUP
condition: triage policy
age_group: general
urgency_tag: home
is_danger_sign: false
---
Use HOME_CARE_WITH_FOLLOWUP when there is NO danger sign and the condition is
within ASHA scope to manage at home: simple cough or cold, uncomplicated fever in
an alert child who is drinking, or diarrhoea with no dehydration. Action: give
protocol home care (fluids, ORS/zinc for diarrhoea, feeding advice), teach the
danger signs, and arrange a follow-up (commonly in 2 days) with clear return
advice.

---
Use ROUTINE_HEALTH_EDUCATION when there is no acute illness and the contact is for
prevention or counselling: routine antenatal care, immunization, nutrition,
hygiene, or general advice. Action: provide health education and link the person
to routine services.

### CHUNK
source: ASHA Sahayak Triage Guidance
section: Conservative default when protocols do not cover the case
condition: triage policy
age_group: general
urgency_tag: urgent
is_danger_sign: false
---
If the retrieved protocols do not clearly cover the case, or critical information
is missing and cannot be obtained, do NOT guess. Default to referral
(URGENT_REFER_TODAY or higher) and state that the protocols available do not cover
the case. A false alarm is acceptable; a missed emergency is not.
