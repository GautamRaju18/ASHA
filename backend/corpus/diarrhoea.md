# IMNCI — Diarrhoea and Dehydration

> Starter corpus, paraphrased from public IMNCI diarrhoea classification content.

### CHUNK
source: IMNCI Chart Booklet (Diarrhoea)
section: Severe dehydration
condition: diarrhoea
age_group: child
urgency_tag: emergency
is_danger_sign: true
---
Classify dehydration in a child with diarrhoea. SEVERE DEHYDRATION is present if
TWO or more of these signs are seen: the child is lethargic or unconscious; sunken
eyes; not able to drink or drinking poorly; or a skin pinch goes back very slowly
(2 seconds or more). Severe dehydration is an EMERGENCY. Give fluids urgently
(IV/ORS per protocol within scope) and refer URGENTLY to hospital.

---
SOME DEHYDRATION is present if TWO or more of these signs are seen: the child is
restless or irritable; sunken eyes; drinks eagerly or is thirsty; or a skin pinch
goes back slowly. A child with some dehydration needs ORS solution and zinc, and
should be observed and reassessed; refer the same day if not improving or if any
danger sign appears.

### CHUNK
source: IMNCI Chart Booklet (Diarrhoea)
section: No dehydration — home care with ORS and zinc
condition: diarrhoea
age_group: child
urgency_tag: home
is_danger_sign: false
---
A child with diarrhoea who is alert, drinking normally, with no sunken eyes and a
normal skin pinch has NO DEHYDRATION and can be managed at home. Give extra
fluids and ORS solution after each loose stool, give zinc supplements for 10–14
days, continue feeding/breastfeeding, and advise the caregiver to return
immediately if the child becomes lethargic, cannot drink or breastfeed, has sunken
eyes, has blood in the stool, or the diarrhoea worsens. Diarrhoea lasting 14 days
or more (persistent diarrhoea) or blood in the stool (dysentery) needs referral.
