# Common Conditions within ASHA Scope + IDSP Notifiable Flags

> Starter corpus, paraphrased from public ASHA module / IDSP guidance.

### CHUNK
source: ASHA Module (Acute respiratory infection — no pneumonia)
section: Cough or cold without pneumonia
condition: common cold
age_group: general
urgency_tag: home
is_danger_sign: false
---
A person (child or adult) with cough or a runny nose for a few days who is alert,
feeding/eating and drinking normally, has NO fast breathing, NO chest indrawing,
and NO danger sign most likely has a simple cough or cold (no pneumonia). Manage
at home: soothe the throat with safe home remedies, encourage fluids, continue
feeding, and clear a blocked nose. No antibiotic is needed. Advise return if
breathing becomes fast or difficult, the person cannot drink, or symptoms last
more than 14 days.

### CHUNK
source: ASHA Module (Health education)
section: Routine health education and prevention
condition: prevention
age_group: general
urgency_tag: routine
is_danger_sign: false
---
For people with no acute illness, provide routine health education: hand washing,
safe drinking water, nutrition and exclusive breastfeeding for infants under 6
months, immunization schedule, use of ORS and zinc for diarrhoea, and recognising
danger signs that need a health facility. Encourage timely use of the sub-centre,
PHC, and immunization sessions.

### CHUNK
source: IDSP (Integrated Disease Surveillance Programme)
section: Notifiable / outbreak-alert conditions
condition: notifiable disease
age_group: general
urgency_tag: urgent
is_danger_sign: true
---
Certain presentations should be reported and escalated under disease
surveillance: acute watery diarrhoea with severe dehydration or clustering of
cases (possible cholera); fever with rash (possible measles); jaundice clusters;
acute flaccid paralysis in a child (possible polio); suspected dengue (high fever
with bleeding, severe abdominal pain, persistent vomiting); and any unusual
clustering of severe illness or deaths. Refer the patient for care and notify the
PHC/health authority promptly.

### CHUNK
source: ASHA Module (Adult general danger signs)
section: General danger signs in adults
condition: general
age_group: adult
urgency_tag: emergency
is_danger_sign: true
---
Refer any adult URGENTLY who has: chest pain or severe breathlessness; sudden
weakness of the face or limbs, difficulty speaking, or facial droop (possible
stroke); unconsciousness or confusion; convulsions; severe bleeding; severe
dehydration; very high or persistent fever with confusion or stiff neck; or
coughing blood. These require emergency referral to a CHC or district hospital
and, where appropriate, the 108 ambulance service.
